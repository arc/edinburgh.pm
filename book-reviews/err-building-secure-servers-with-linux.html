Buildling Secure Servers with Linux
AuthorMichael D. Bauer
PublisherO'Reilly
ISBN0-596-00217-3
DateOctober 2002
Pages430
Price£31.95
ReviewerRory Macdonald

The opening chapter sketches out some risk modeling techniques.

Uninspiring and unengaging writing style makes this a real bore to wade through.

While dealing with securing MTAs the author chooses only to cover Sendmail and Postfix, leaving out major players like Exim and qmail. I believe this to be to the detriment of the text, especially in light of the author's basis for including Postfix - it's the MTA on his own servers.

Not even the half page flame-avoiding disclaimer does anything to replace the void.

Summary

While "Data Munging with Perl" is not presented as a traditional reference text, it tackles data munging problems cleanly enough to serve as one for the issues touched opon.

The rear cover blurb suggests that "this book will save you time", and on the grounds of it's sensible suggestions for the design of data and code, I can't argue too much with that.

For newcomers to perl I would suggest "Data Munging with Perl" as perhaps a 3rd or 4th perl text, and unless you are already confident of your munging capabilities then this text deserves a place on your bookshelf.
